Butch Cassidy

Real Name: Robert Leroy Parker

Born: April 13, 1866, Beaver, Utah, USA

Butch Cassidy is best known as the leader of the Wild Bunch, a gang of outlaws that included other famous criminals like the Sundance Kid (Harry Longabaugh). Butch was charismatic and relatively principled among outlaws, often preferring robbery over violence. He was born into a Mormon family in Utah, and his first major brush with the law was at a young age when he journeyed far from home and returned wearing clothes he couldn't afford. In the late 1890s, the Wild Bunch was responsible for a series of bold and highly publicized bank and train robberies across the West. What made Cassidy notable was his strategic approach to crime, including the use of thorough planning and non-violent methods whenever possible. For instance, he famously persuaded nervous hostages to sing during a robbery to calm them down. Facing increased pressure from law enforcement, Butch Cassidy, along with the Sundance Kid and Etta Place, fled to South America around 1901. Their fate is somewhat shrouded in mystery, but it is widely believed that Butch Cassidy died in Bolivia in 1908, following a shootout with the local authorities.

Billy the kid

Real Name: Henry McCarty (also known as William H. Bonney)

Born: November 23, 1859, New York City, USA

Billy the Kid is one of the most mythologized figures in the Old West, notorious for his reputed killing of 21 men—one for each year of his life, though the actual number is likely lower. Born in New York City, he moved west with his family, eventually ending up in New Mexico. His criminal career included cattle rustling, theft, and participation in the Lincoln County War, a violent conflict between rival merchant factions in New Mexico. Billy's knack for escape and evasion made him a legendary figure; he famously escaped from jail multiple times, once killing two deputies during one of these escapes. Billy the Kid was known for his deadly skill with a gun and his youthful appearance, which belied a ruthless survival instinct. His life came to an end when he was shot by Sheriff Pat Garrett in Fort Sumner, New Mexico, on July 14, 1881. Despite his violent reputation, Billy was also known to be charming and charismatic, traits that contributed to his folk hero status.
